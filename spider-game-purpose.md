# Spider Game Purpose

The spider mini-game is a quiet, playful metaphor for building systems on the web that can also be beautiful, delightful, and worth spending time with.

The visitor clicks the portrait and discovers a small hidden interaction where a spider turns motion into structure. Each catch leaves something behind. What first looks like a toy becomes a tiny demonstration of the site's core idea: making useful systems out of fragments, momentum, constraints, craft, beauty, and delight.

The game should communicate:

- Craft: the site was made by someone who cares about small interaction details.
- Systems thinking: every hook creates a connection; every connection becomes part of a larger structure.
- Beauty and delight: systems on the web should not only work; they can feel elegant, generous, and quietly magical.
- Web / website pun: "spin a web" works literally and conceptually.
- Playfulness without spectacle: it rewards curiosity, but does not hijack the portfolio.
- Transformation: chaotic motion becomes an elegant structure.

The game should not feel like:

- A separate arcade feature.
- A gimmick unrelated to the page.
- A challenge that blocks reading.
- A loud "look at me" animation.

North star:

> The spider is not just surviving. It is making the page more connected.

It is also making the page more beautiful.

Design rule:

> Every interaction should leave a subtle trace of construction. The win state is not score; it is emergence.
